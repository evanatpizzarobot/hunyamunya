Yenn

§ Profile · HMR

Luxembourg producer and YARecords founder whose breakthrough What I Feel (HMR009, 2008) on Hunya Munya Records arrived as a 12" with remixes by Evan Michael and Fabian Schumann, later earning praise from Paul van Dyk, Carl Cox, and Markus Schulz.

Rated highly by some of the world’s top DJs and producers, Yenn has built an impressive career as both an artist and label owner, all before the age of 30. With a relentless drive to innovate and improve, the Luxembourg-born producer and DJ has established himself as one of electronic music’s rising international talents.

Yenn began producing in 1997, experimenting with everything from high-energy dance and classical to video game music, experiences that laid the foundation for his versatile sound. By 2002 he had taken up DJing, giving him a platform to share his productions and connect with audiences.

In 2007, Yenn launched YARecords with his debut single My Secret Love. The following year, he broke through internationally with What I Feel on the American label Hunya Munya Records, an anthemic electro track that attracted global attention and led to remix work for Bonzai Music. His remix quickly gained acclaim, with legendary acts Dumonde and Yves Deruyter naming it one of the best remixes of the year.

Momentum continued with the release of his It So Sux EP on Miles Dyson’s Plasmapool Records in 2008. Shortly after, Yenn rebranded his label as Noobish Records, setting the tone with a remix package featuring electro heavyweights Electrixx. Noobish quickly grew into a respected outlet for forward-thinking electro, with standout releases including Yenn’s reissued What I Felt and his powerful remix of Space DJz, Tabula Rasa, a collaboration born after his live set at Luxembourg’s biggest festival impressed the Space DJz themselves.

Collaborations continued to elevate his profile, including a mutual remix exchange with Canadian electro star Lazy Rich (Noobish Records / Big Fish Recordings). His own tracks such as Oozing and Starz (Jigsaw Recordings) gained heavy rotation from global icons like Paul van Dyk, Carl Cox, Markus Schulz, and Lange, while Asa Daschi (Plasmapool), remixed by Miles Dyson, climbed high on the Beatport charts.

Regularly featured on major digital platforms such as Beatport and Trackitdown, Yenn continues to release chart-topping tracks while pushing Noobish Records forward. His recent collaboration with rising electro house and breaks producer Refracture on Symphony is already making waves, and with more high-profile collaborations and releases in the pipeline, Yenn shows no signs of slowing down.
