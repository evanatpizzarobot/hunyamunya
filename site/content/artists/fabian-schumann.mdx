<p data-start="228" data-end="478">German producer <strong data-start="244" data-end="263">Fabian Schumann</strong> discovered his passion for electronic music at just 13 years old. By 17, he was the youngest participant at the 2003 Goliath Festival in Schwerin, North Germany — an early signal of the career that was to follow.</p>
<p data-start="480" data-end="890">Driven by a desire to push beyond established styles, Fabian soon began producing his own music, experimenting across genres and carving out a distinctive voice. He first gained recognition with his live project <em data-start="692" data-end="707">Electrostatic</em>, before launching a solo career in 2006. That July, his debut EP <em data-start="773" data-end="792">Wir Wollen Tanzen</em> was released on <strong data-start="809" data-end="836">Klang Gymnastik Records</strong>, marking the beginning of his professional catalog.</p>
<p data-start="892" data-end="1317">His breakthrough came in February 2006 with the track <em data-start="946" data-end="962">We Are So High</em>, released alongside artists such as Stereofunk, Kn?e, and Vasco &amp; Jersey. Known for his experimental minimal productions, Fabian quickly established himself as both a producer and remixer, reworking projects for multiple acts — including Berlin duo <strong data-start="1212" data-end="1225">Electrixx</strong> in 2007. He also appeared on the Berlin label <strong data-start="1272" data-end="1289">Suicide Robot</strong> with the <em data-start="1299" data-end="1311">Tokio Rush</em> EP.</p>
<p data-start="1319" data-end="1663">In 2007, renowned DJ <strong data-start="1340" data-end="1356">Robbespierre</strong> took notice of Fabian, leading to the launch of the collaborative project <strong data-start="1431" data-end="1451">Schumann &amp; Kloss</strong>. Their debut, <em data-start="1466" data-end="1477">Snoize EP</em>, was released on <strong data-start="1495" data-end="1512">Majestic Trax</strong> later that year, followed by high-profile remix work in 2008 for artists such as <strong data-start="1594" data-end="1624">Marksun &amp; Brian (Euphonic)</strong> and <strong data-start="1629" data-end="1660">Solar Lords (Majestic Trax)</strong>.</p>
<p data-start="1665" data-end="2087">Fabian’s international profile rose further with his single <em data-start="1725" data-end="1733">Enjomo</em> on Luxembourg’s <strong data-start="1750" data-end="1763">YARecords</strong>, run by Yannick Ciancanelli — solidifying his reputation as a rising voice in European minimal and progressive house. Today, Fabian continues to refine his sound, with his latest work featured on <strong data-start="1960" data-end="1983">Hunya Munya Records</strong>, showcasing a producer who has grown from youthful curiosity into an artist with international reach.</p>
