<p>The Beginning</p>

<p>Some people are undoubtedly born more creative than others, but whether or<br />not they're destined to channel their creativity through a specific art<br />form is questionable. Øistein Johan Eide (aka Boom Jinx) did not come from<br />a particularly musical family. The fact that his creativity manifested<br />itself in sound and music was pure coincidence. He started making music in<br />1989 when he accidentally got his hands on Soundtracker - a pattern based<br />audio sequencer for the Commodore Amiga computer. In terms of audio<br />specifications, the Amiga was basically an 8 bit 4 voice sampler. With a<br />burning passion to create, 15-year-old Øistein spent more time making<br />music than he did attending Norwegian elementary school. When he got his<br />first freelance assignment from a major US game developer in 1991, he set<br />a goal on making a career out of making music.</p>

<p>Milestones</p>

<p>By 2003, Øistein had produced music for more than 50 commercials. His work<br />was being exposed to millions of people worldwide through international<br />commercials and websites for high-profile brands and personalities. At the<br />height of his freelance career, he was headhunted and offered full-time<br />employment at one of the leading sound and music production companies in<br />Norway. These days, it's easier for Øistein to say what type of projects<br />he hasn't worked on than what he has, and his music airs on TV, Cinema and<br />Radio every day.</p>

<p>Birth of an Alter Ego</p>

<p>Up until recently, when asked what style of music he makes, he would<br />usually reply "whatever the client asks for", but like any other artist he<br />had his preferences. An artists preferred style is often shaped by their<br />specific talents. Øistein has never been afraid to admit his specialty is<br />more technically oriented than that of a stereotypical composer. While<br />recognizing how fortunate he was to make a living doing what he loved, he<br />also realized he would rarely be able to express himself with complete<br />creative freedom due to the restrictions of clients' needs. And thus, Boom<br />Jinx was born.</p>

<p>The Present</p>

<p>Despite a limited number of releases since Boom Jinx's inception in 2005,<br />Øistein has developed a well deserved reputation amongst his peers for<br />productions that showcase a stunning level of intricate detail and subtle<br />nuances. His tracks are equally at home on the dance floor as they are in<br />your headphones, getting support from industry heavyweights such as<br />Tiësto, Paul Van Dyk, Armin Van Buuren, Above &amp; Beyond and Marco V. He has<br />also received critical acclaim from DJ Magazine and URB who both gave<br />glowing reviews for "Come Play Perfect" and "Remember September."<br />Considering that his tracks have achieved top placement in European dance<br />charts and gotten consistent play on worldwide radio shows, it is clear<br />that his music resonates with the masses as well. Øistein's musical talent<br />is far from one dimensional and not limited to the trance scene. Not only<br />was his breakbeat production "Manipulator" premiered on BBC Radio 1 as a<br />"hot new track" alongside Nelly Furtado, it was also licensed by the hit<br />television show CSI: Miami. With his debut album in the works, Boom Jinx<br />is poised to leave a lasting impression on listeners all over the globe.</p>

<p>By Jeera Lert aka DJ Thaifoon — June 30, 2007</p>
