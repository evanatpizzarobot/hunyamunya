<p>Rated highly by some of the world’s top DJs and producers, <strong>Yenn</strong> has built an impressive career as both an artist and label owner — all before the age of 30. With a relentless drive to innovate and improve, the Luxembourg-born producer and DJ has established himself as one of electronic music’s rising international talents.</p>

<p>Yenn began producing in 1997, experimenting with everything from high-energy dance and classical to video game music — experiences that laid the foundation for his versatile sound. By 2002 he had taken up DJing, giving him a platform to share his productions and connect with audiences.</p>

<p>In 2007, Yenn launched <strong>YARecords</strong> with his debut single <em>My Secret Love</em>. The following year, he broke through internationally with <em>What I Feel</em> on the American label <strong>Hunya Munya Records</strong>, an anthemic electro track that attracted global attention and led to remix work for <strong>Bonzai Music</strong>. His remix quickly gained acclaim, with legendary acts <strong>Dumonde</strong> and <strong>Yves Deruyter</strong> naming it one of the best remixes of the year.</p>

<p>Momentum continued with the release of his <em>It So Sux</em> EP on <strong>Miles Dyson’s Plasmapool Records</strong> in 2008. Shortly after, Yenn rebranded his label as <strong>Noobish Records</strong>, setting the tone with a remix package featuring electro heavyweights <strong>Electrixx</strong>. Noobish quickly grew into a respected outlet for forward-thinking electro, with standout releases including Yenn’s reissued <em>What I Felt</em> and his powerful remix of <strong>Space DJz – Tabula Rasa</strong>, a collaboration born after his live set at Luxembourg’s biggest festival impressed the Space DJz themselves.</p>

<p>Collaborations continued to elevate his profile, including a mutual remix exchange with Canadian electro star <strong>Lazy Rich</strong> (Noobish Records / Big Fish Recordings). His own tracks such as <em>Oozing</em> and <em>Starz</em> (Jigsaw Recordings) gained heavy rotation from global icons like <strong>Paul van Dyk, Carl Cox, Markus Schulz,</strong> and <strong>Lange</strong>, while <em>Asa Daschi</em> (Plasmapool) — remixed by Miles Dyson — climbed high on the Beatport charts.</p>

<p>Regularly featured on major digital platforms such as Beatport and Trackitdown, Yenn continues to release chart-topping tracks while pushing Noobish Records forward. His recent collaboration with rising electro house and breaks producer <strong>Refracture</strong> on <em>Symphony</em> is already making waves — and with more high-profile collaborations and releases in the pipeline, Yenn shows no signs of slowing down.</p>
