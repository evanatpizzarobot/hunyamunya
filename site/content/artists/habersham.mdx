<p>At just twenty years old, <strong>Damon Fonooni</strong> — known worldwide as <strong>Habersham</strong> — has already achieved what many artists spend a lifetime pursuing. Born in Iran and raised across multiple countries, he now calls Atlanta, Georgia home, balancing a full-time academic schedule with a rapidly accelerating music career.</p>

<p>A DJ for over five years and producer for three, Habersham has never limited his vision to the local scene. Instead, his sights have always been set on the global stage — a goal that is quickly becoming reality. His original productions and remixes have earned support from some of the biggest names in electronic music, including <strong>Craig Richards, Saeed &amp; Palash, Barry Gilbey, Sander Kleinenberg, Martin Villeneuve (MV/Envy), Chris Fortier, Tim Skinner, Jimmy Van M, Koma &amp; Bones, Ben Camp, Paolo Mojo, Jason Jollins, Hector Romero, Gene Carbonell,</strong> and <strong>Phil K</strong>.</p>

<p>Phil K notably licensed Habersham’s single <em>Mister Cool</em> (Music Is Freedom) for his acclaimed <em>Balance 004</em> compilation, further cementing Habersham’s reputation as a rising force in progressive and forward-thinking dance music. Another of his tracks was also featured in Phil K’s guest mix on <strong>John Digweed’s Kiss FM show</strong> in December, exposing his work to a worldwide audience.</p>

<p>With international recognition growing at a remarkable pace, Habersham stands as one of the most promising new talents in electronic music — an artist driven not just by skill, but by vision, versatility, and a relentless passion for sound.</p>
