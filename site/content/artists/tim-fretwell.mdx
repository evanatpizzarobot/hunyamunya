<p>Born and raised in Sheffield, England, <strong>Tim Fretwell</strong> was first introduced to electronic music in the early ’90s through pioneering acts such as The Prodigy, Altern-8, and Moby. By the mid-’90s, he had turned his hand to production, drawing influence from the deep, sultry house sounds of artists like <strong>Deep Dish</strong>. His earliest breakthrough came when <strong>Computer Music Magazine</strong> published one of his demos on their cover CD, accompanied by a glowing review. The exposure led to interest from several labels, including the emerging <strong>Climax Recordings</strong> in Northern England, who signed his tracks <em>Where to Next?</em> and <em>Glide</em>. Both releases climbed into the Balance charts Top 20 and gained support from top DJs worldwide.</p>

<p>Fretwell’s profile grew further when he was commissioned to remix <strong>Paul Lancaster’s Bad Girl</strong>, a deep and sensual breaks rework that caught the attention of <strong>John Digweed</strong>, who featured it on his <strong>Kiss FM</strong> show. The remix also won praise from <strong>Sander Kleinenberg</strong> and <strong>James Zabiela</strong>, leading to a direct request from Digweed for remix duties on <strong>Bedrock Records</strong>, tackling the massive <em>Pole Folder &amp; CP, Dust</em>.</p>

<p>From there, Tim went on to remix some of the scene’s most respected names, including <strong>Steve Porter, Hyper, Momu, Pole Folder,</strong> and <strong>Bedrock</strong> themselves. His original productions also drew acclaim, beginning with <em>Ember</em> (Electrofly Breaks) and continuing with releases such as <em>Faceless</em>, <em>Dreams Attack</em>, <em>Fill Me Up</em>, and <em>Silky Dirty</em> on <strong>Baroque Records</strong>. These tracks received widespread international support, multiple plays on Digweed’s <strong>Kiss FM</strong> show, and airplay on <strong>BBC Radio 1</strong>. His music has also featured on television (Channel 4 UK) and appeared on high-profile compilations including <strong>Anthony Pappa’s Balance CD</strong>, <strong>Bedrock’s Layered Sounds 2</strong>, <strong>Chris Salt’s Future House</strong>, and <strong>Global Underground’s Electric Calm 2</strong>.</p>

<p>With a career defined by both critically acclaimed originals and standout remixes, Tim Fretwell has established himself as a producer whose deep, melodic, and forward-thinking sound continues to resonate with dance floors and tastemakers around the world.</p>
